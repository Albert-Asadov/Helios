Buttons include a text label and can optionally include icons. They come in various shapes, sizes, and colors to draw attention. Buttons have different states like normal, hover, active,and disabled.

Best practice

Clear labels: Use concise and descriptive button labels. Placement: Position buttons where users expect to find them. Size: Ensure buttons are large enough for easy clicking or tapping. Styling: Use visual cues to distinguish buttons from other elements. Consistency: Maintain a consistent design style for buttons. Affordance: Make buttons visually indicate their interactive nature. Feedback: Provide visual feedback when buttons are clicked or tapped. Accessibility: Ensure buttons are accessible to users with disabilities. Hierarchy: Prioritize buttons based on importance and frequency of use. Testing: Test button designs with real users for feedback and improvements.
